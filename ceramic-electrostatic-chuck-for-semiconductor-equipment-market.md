<p><p>The Ceramic Electrostatic Chuck (ESC) for Semiconductor Equipment market consists of two main types: Coulomb Type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type. The Coulomb Type uses an electrostatic force of attraction between opposing charges to secure the semiconductor wafer. It relies on the principle of positive and negative charges. On the other hand, the JR Type utilizes the phenomenon of electrostatic levitation to hold the wafer in place, by creating a vacuum environment and inducing an electrostatic force. Both types of ESCs are essential components in semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>

<p><p>Hypoparathyroidism is a rare endocrine disorder caused by an inadequate production of parathyroid hormone (PTH). The primary goal of hypoparathyroidism treatment is to control symptoms and manage complications associated with low levels of PTH. Currently, there is no cure for hypoparathyroidism, so treatment mainly focuses on addressing calcium and vitamin D imbalances in the body.</p><p>The most common treatment approach for hypoparathyroidism includes oral calcium and vitamin D supplements to maintain normal levels of these nutrients in the blood. In severe cases, patients may require intravenous calcium supplements as well. However, long-term use of high-dose calcium can increase the risk of kidney stones and other complications. Therefore, newer treatment approaches aim to restore PTH levels through the use of recombinant PTH therapy, which has shown promising results in clinical trials.</p><p>The hypoparathyroidism treatment market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><ul><li>Oral</li><li>Parenteral</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hypoparathyroidism treatment market includes two main types: oral and parenteral. Oral medications are taken by mouth and help to replace the inadequate levels of parathyroid hormone (PTH) in the body. These medications usually contain a form of PTH or its derivative. On the other hand, parenteral treatment involves delivering PTH through injections or infusions directly into the bloodstream. This method is often used in cases where oral medications are not effective or not well-tolerated. Both types of treatment aim to restore the balance of calcium and phosphate in the body for managing hypoparathyroidism symptoms.</p></p>
